# THE-CURIOSITY-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-CURRICULUM

On the epistemic architecture of children's play: why curiosity-driven information-gain, not developmental feature claims, was always the active ingredient the toy aisle never measured, and what the 2026 literature now lets us say with precision about pretend play, offline consolidation, neural synchrony, and the economics of prediction error.

## Opening

THE-PREDICTION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-PLAY established three things the original playtritionism document had left informal: that THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY of a play environment is the Shannon-formal measure of what the toy aisle has never put on a label; that THE-TURN-STRUCTURE, not word count, is the unit of language development; and that THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW — the band of prediction error that is positive enough to be informative and tractable enough to be reduced — is the zone play must hit to generate genuine developmental yield. Those three constructs gave the playtritionism thesis a substrate it had been missing.

Three gaps remained, each of which the 2025–2026 literature has since filled with enough resolution to warrant a third document.

First: THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW was named and placed within the active inference framework, but its internal structure was not specified. What is the functional form of the window? How does it vary across children? What is its relationship to individual differences in curiosity — differences that are now, for the first time, measurable in 8-month-olds and shown to predict IQ at 3.5 years? The ideal-learner literature (Kidd et al., 2012; de Boer et al., 2025) has provided that specification, and the specification changes the prediction structure of the theory.

Second: THE-TURN-STRUCTURE was identified as the causal unit of language development, and THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE beneath it — the dyadic neural coupling that makes contingent exchange a physical learning event rather than a merely informational one — was implied by the active inference account but never named. The 2024–2026 hyperscanning literature (Eulau & Hirsh-Pasek, 2024; Bánki et al., 2023) has made the substrate visible. An electronic toy that eliminates THE-TURN-STRUCTURE is not just removing a linguistic event; it is eliminating a synchronized neural coupling event. The loss is not semantic; it is oscillatory.

Third: the second document addressed pretend play nowhere, despite the fact that pretend play represents the largest single block of discretionary time in early childhood and is the play type most systematically displaced by feature-engineered toys. The 2025 literature has closed the pretend-play gap in a specific way: by establishing that pretend play and counterfactual causal reasoning share a cognitive architecture (Francis & Gibson, 2023) and that pretend play is, under the active inference account, not a departure from the prediction-error framework but an extension of it — the child running the generative model offline, in pure hypothetical mode, without physical sensory feedback. Call this THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE.

This document provides the substrate for all three gaps. It introduces eight new formal constructs — THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT, THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR, THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE, THE-WAKE-WINDOW, THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE, THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION, THE-RISKY-SURPLUS, and THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION — and maps them against the 2025–2026 experimental record. It adds thirteen new formal correspondences to the playtritionism table and eight novel falsifiable predictions extending the prior document's eight. It does not revise the playtritionism thesis. It sharpens it to the point where the experiments that would falsify it can be precisely described.

## I. What the Prior Documents Left Without Substrate

The prior documents produced a sequence of increasingly formal claims about play:

Document One established the nutritionism frame and named the matrix. Document Two formalized the matrix as THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY, named THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW as the developmental zone, identified THE-TURN-STRUCTURE as the linguistic mechanism, and introduced THE-DIRECTED-SURPLUS and THE-ASSEMBLY-GAP as the formal expressions of what unstructured play builds that no feature can substitute for.

But four gaps remained, each visible in retrospect:

**Gap 1: The functional form of THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW was unspecified.** The prior document said the window exists, that electronic toys sit below it, and that a loose-parts set of appropriate size sits inside it. It did not say what the mathematical relationship between complexity and engagement looks like. The ideal-learner literature, formalized in Kidd, Piantadosi, and Aslin (2012), provides that specification: the probability of sustained attention follows an inverted-U over the complexity axis, with the peak at intermediate complexity defined by the child's current internal model. This is not a metaphor. It is a model with quantifiable parameters.

**Gap 2: THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR was unacknowledged.** If the window exists and has a functional form, then individual differences in where the peak is located, and in how sensitive the child is to information gain, become central variables. De Boer et al. (2025) show for the first time that individual differences in information-gain sensitivity at 8 months predict IQ at 3.5 years. The implication for the playtritionism argument is that the same environment — the same block set with the same part-count — places some children inside THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW and others outside it. The toy market treats this as a segmentation problem (age-graded products). The data suggest it is a curiosity-vector problem that age-grading systematically misaddresses.

**Gap 3: THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE under THE-TURN-STRUCTURE was unnamed.** The prior document described THE-TURN-STRUCTURE as a conversational event. It is also a neural event. Eulau and Hirsh-Pasek (2024) synthesize the EEG/fNIRS hyperscanning literature to show that caregiver-child behavioral synchrony during joint attention episodes produces synchronized activity in the temporoparietal regions that support language processing in both nervous systems simultaneously. An electronic toy that occupies both sides of the conversational turn is not just eliminating a behavioral event; it is eliminating a synchronized neural event. THE-RESOLUTION-RACE has a neural cost that word-count studies never captured.

**Gap 4: Pretend play was missing entirely.** The prior documents focused on constructive, physical play with manipulable objects. Pretend play — the replacement of the object's physical identity with a hypothetical one, the "this stick is a spaceship" transform — received no account. The 2023–2025 literature provides one: pretend play and counterfactual causal reasoning share a latent construct (r = .51; Francis & Gibson, 2023), both requiring the child to hold a hypothetical world-model simultaneously with the actual one. Under the active inference account, this is the child running THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW not on physical affordances but on narrative inconsistency. The prediction-error signal in pretend play is not "will this block hold?" but "wait, spaceships can't fit through doors" — a counterfactual prediction error that updates the child's causal model of the hypothetical world in exactly the same way that construction errors update the causal model of the physical one.

## II. THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT: The Functional Form THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW Was Always Describing

Kidd, Piantadosi, and Aslin (2012) measured 7- and 8-month-olds' looking behavior across stimulus sequences varying in algorithmic complexity — operationalized as the negative log-probability of each element given the infant's current learned model of the sequence. The result was a U-shaped disengagement function: the probability of looking away was highest at complexity extremes (too predictable or too surprising) and lowest at intermediate complexity. The functional form is an inverted-U over the complexity axis.

Call this THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT: the mathematical relationship between environmental complexity (measured in bits relative to the child's internal model) and sustained engagement. The gradient has three regions:

**Below the window:** prediction error is too low. The child has already modeled the environment and generates no new error signal. Engagement terminates. This is the condition an electronic toy with a fixed output produces after the first few presses: the child has modeled "press → light," and THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT pushes attention elsewhere.

**Inside the window:** prediction error is intermediate and tractable. The environment is regular enough to model but uncertain enough to motivate modeling. Engagement is sustained. Error cycles run. Generative model updates occur. This is the condition a loose-parts set of appropriate complexity and a well-matched part-count produces continuously, as long as the child has not exhausted the configuration space.

**Above the window:** prediction error is too high. The child cannot model the environment within their current representational capacity. Error is irresolvable. Engagement terminates — not because the child is bored but because the task is beyond their working model. This is the condition a set of four hundred identical blocks placed before a 22-month-old produces.

THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT provides the mathematical substrate THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW was always implying. It also resolves a puzzle the prior document left open: why does THE-LOOSE-PARTS-SATURATION-CEILING exist? Not primarily because of working-memory limits on the number of blocks a child can hold in mind, but because as part-count increases, the configuration space's average complexity (relative to the child's model) increases until it exceeds the upper boundary of THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT. More parts eventually put the session above the window. The saturation ceiling is the inflection point on the high-complexity side of the U.

THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT also formalizes what the original document called "the toy that always wins THE-RESOLUTION-RACE." A toy with a fixed output asymptotically approaches the left edge of THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT after a small number of interactions. Not because it is simple, but because it is predictable — it has a low expected information gain. The child's model of the toy converges fast, and once converged, the toy can generate no further learning signal regardless of how sophisticated its electronics.

## III. THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR: The Individual-Difference Moderator THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT Was Missing

THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT is specified relative to a child's internal model. Two children with different internal models — different priors, different curiosity sensitivities, different rates of model updating — will have different peaks on the gradient for the same stimulus. This is not a peripheral finding. It is the central moderator variable the playtritionism argument needed but never had.

De Boer, Poli, Meyer, Mars, and Hunnius (2025; Developmental Science) conducted the first longitudinal study connecting individual differences in infants' information-gain sensitivity to later cognitive development. The design: 8-month-olds were presented with stimuli varying in information-gain opportunity (operationalized via eye-tracking looking behavior). Sensitivity to information gain was measured as the degree to which infants preferentially directed attention toward information-rich over information-poor stimuli. IQ was assessed at 3.5 years. The result: infants in the top third of curiosity-driven information-gain sensitivity at 8 months showed significantly higher IQ scores at 3.5 years. The association was non-linear — the effect concentrated in the highest-curiosity group, not uniformly across the distribution.

Call the variable this study quantifies THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR: the individual's sensitivity to information gain, operationalized as the degree to which attention is preferentially directed toward stimuli that will reduce uncertainty in the current internal model. THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR is not the same as general attentiveness, novelty preference, or habituation rate. De Boer et al. (2025) establish that habituation speed — the rate at which an infant stops attending to a repeated stimulus — is unrelated to information-gain sensitivity. Two children who habituate at the same rate can have radically different THE-CURIOSITY-VECTORs, because habituation reflects processing speed while THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR reflects the preference for states that update the generative model.

The implications for the playtritionism argument are precise:

**Implication 1:** The effective configuration space of a play environment is not a property of the environment alone. It is a product of the environment's objective information structure and the child's THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR. A child with a high THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR extracts more developmental yield from the same block set, because they are more sensitive to the prediction-error signals the set generates. A toy marketed on a feature claim — which pre-resolves prediction errors regardless of THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR — delivers the same developmental yield (approximately zero new model updates after the first few interactions) regardless of whether the child has a high or low THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR.

**Implication 2:** THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT shifts with THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR. High-curiosity children will engage sustainably with configurations of higher complexity than low-curiosity children of the same age. The age-graded toy market assumes THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT shifts uniformly with age. The de Boer et al. data suggest it varies substantially within-age based on THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR, which itself appears stable as early as 8 months and predictive of cognitive outcomes years later. An age-appropriate toy is a more heterogeneous concept than the product category implies.

**Implication 3:** THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR may be educable. Caunt, Lee, and colleagues (2026; Developmental Science) establish that state curiosity — curiosity induced by contextual framing — drives word learning and information seeking in children, and that curiosity-driven exploration is mediated by expected information gain, not just novelty. If state curiosity can be induced by environment, then the design of the play environment — its open-endedness, the parent's epistemic behavior, the absence of pre-resolved outcomes — may shape not just the session's yield but the child's trait-level THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR over developmental time. The toy that pre-resolves the prediction error is not just failing to produce yield from the current session. It may be training the child's THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR downward.

## IV. THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE: Pretend Play as Offline Generative Modeling

The active inference account of play, as formalized by Friston and colleagues (2017) and extended by the active inference education synthesis (2024), concerns itself with online prediction-error reduction: the child acts on an object, generates a prediction error, updates the generative model. What the framework implies about pretend play — and what neither the prior documents nor the Friston synthesis made explicit — is that pretend play is the offline version of the same operation.

When a child holds a block and says "this is a spaceship," they are not failing to distinguish fantasy from reality, and they are not confusedly attributing spaceship properties to a block. They are running a hypothetical: treating a propositioned counterfactual world-state as if it were actual, generating predictions from it, and then acting within that hypothetical world in ways that produce prediction errors. The block-spaceship "cannot fit through the cardboard door" — prediction error. The spaceship needs fuel before it can land — model update. The mission is completed when the child reaches an internal criterion — a state in which the hypothetical world has resolved its open prediction errors.

Francis and Gibson (2023; British Journal of Psychology) tested the cognitive architecture of this correspondence directly. Using confirmatory factor analysis across 189 children aged approximately 4.8 years, they measured pretend play, counterfactual reasoning, executive function, and language. Pretend play and counterfactual reasoning loaded onto a single latent construct (r = .51, p = .001), even when controlling for executive function and language. The cognitive operations that constitute pretend play — holding a hypothetical world-model separate from the actual one while drawing inferences from the hypothetical — are formally identical to the operations that constitute counterfactual causal reasoning.

Under the active inference account, this identity is expected: both are cases of running the generative model in an "offline" mode, where the sensory input is not the physical world but a hypothetical propositioned by the agent itself. Call this THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE: the formal claim that pretend play is not a departure from the prediction-error cycle but an extension of it into a domain where the environment is internally simulated rather than physically manipulated.

THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE has a specific prediction for the playtritionism argument: a feature-engineered toy that eliminates online prediction error (by pre-resolving physical outcomes) also eliminates the conditions for productive offline simulation. A child who has not generated physical prediction errors with an object — who has only experienced pre-resolved outcomes — has a generative model of that object that does not contain the affordance structure necessary to support rich pretend play. THE-RESOLUTION-RACE does not just crowd out physical construction; it crowds out the material from which imagination builds. The stick that becomes a spaceship requires a generative model of stick-as-physical-object rich enough to support the counterfactual transformation. A toy whose outputs are always pre-determined cannot accumulate that model.

THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE also formalizes why the 2025 literature on pretend play and theory of mind is consistent with the playtritionism account rather than requiring a separate explanation. The child who holds a hypothetical world-model separate from actual reality — the cognitive operation that constitutes pretend play — is also modeling another agent's internal states. "Pretend you are the monster" requires holding both the child's own world-model and a hypothetical model of the monster's internal states simultaneously. This is not a separate cognitive achievement from THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE; it is an application of the same offline generative modeling capacity to a social object rather than a physical one.

## V. THE-WAKE-WINDOW: Children's Offline Consolidation and What Play Disrupts

The prior document identified THE-CAUSAL-HORIZON as the gap between what the correlational literature establishes and what the causal literature has demonstrated. A finding from the 2026 motor learning literature crosses an adjacent horizon: the mechanism by which play's learning signal is consolidated into the generative model differs fundamentally between children and adults, and the difference has direct implications for what the play session structure should look like.

Van Roy and King (2026; bioRxiv) used multivoxel pattern analysis of functional MRI to compare learning-related patterns of hippocampal activity in children (aged 7–11) and adults (aged 18–30) during the post-learning wake period following a motor sequence learning task. The result: children showed significantly greater persistence of hippocampal learning-related activity into post-task wakefulness than adults. This pattern is consistent with the behavioral literature showing that pre-adolescent children consolidate newly learned motor sequences during wakefulness at rates that in adults require sleep. The child's hippocampus performs, during awake rest following an active learning episode, what the adult hippocampus performs only during sleep.

Call this THE-WAKE-WINDOW: the post-learning period of awake quiescence during which children's hippocampal circuits replay and consolidate the prediction-error signals accumulated during the preceding action episode. THE-WAKE-WINDOW is not a metaphor for "rest time." It is a neural mechanism — hippocampal sharp-wave ripple replay during awake rest — that operates in children with a temporal structure that the adult literature has consistently assigned to slow-wave sleep.

THE-WAKE-WINDOW has a specific and previously unidentified implication for the playtritionism argument. A loose-parts construction session has a natural temporal structure: the child acts (builds, collapses, rebuilds), then pauses (looks at what they built, holds the block without moving, glances at the parent), then acts again. The pause — the unstructured moment between action cycles — may be neurologically essential: it is the moment when THE-WAKE-WINDOW opens and hippocampal consolidation of the preceding action episode begins. A feature-engineered electronic toy that maintains continuous external stimulation — lights, sounds, audio prompts — eliminates the pause. It does not allow THE-WAKE-WINDOW to open. The learning signal from the previous action cycle cannot consolidate before the toy's next prompt initiates the next interaction. THE-RESOLUTION-RACE not only pre-empts the prediction-error signal; it also pre-empts the consolidation window that would have integrated the signal if one had been generated.

This connects to THE-CAUSAL-HORIZON from the prior document in a specific way: the optimal play session structure is not just about what the child does during active periods. It is about the ratio of active action to unstructured pause. A toy designed to maintain maximal engagement — maximal coverage of the child's attention across the session — is, on this account, a toy designed to minimize THE-WAKE-WINDOW. The educational toy market treats continuous engagement as a design goal. THE-WAKE-WINDOW analysis implies it is an anti-learning design feature.

## VI. THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE: Neural Coupling as the Physical Realization of THE-TURN-STRUCTURE

The prior document established that THE-TURN-STRUCTURE — the bidirectional conversational exchange within a five-second window — is the developmental unit of language acquisition, and that electronic toys displace it by occupying both sides of the exchange simultaneously. It did not specify what THE-TURN-STRUCTURE is at the level of neural mechanism. The 2024–2026 hyperscanning literature provides that specification.

Eulau and Hirsh-Pasek (2024; Frontiers in Integrative Neuroscience) synthesize decades of research on caregiver-child behavioral synchrony and document that the synchrony patterns associated with language development — joint attention, contingent responsiveness, coordinated joint engagement — are accompanied by synchronized neural activity, documented via EEG and fNIRS hyperscanning, in the temporoparietal regions that support language in both the infant's and the caregiver's nervous systems simultaneously. The behavioral synchrony that constitutes THE-TURN-STRUCTURE does not just transmit information between two systems; it synchronizes the oscillatory dynamics of the two systems.

Bánki, Köster, Cichy, and Hoehl (2023; Developmental Cognitive Neuroscience) operationalized this directly: when caregivers used communicative signals (eye contact, ostensive cues) during object-sharing episodes with 12-month-old infants, infant and caregiver SSVEP responses increased in the visual processing regions tracking the shared object. Neural processing of the same object was enhanced in both nervous systems simultaneously when the communicative exchange was active. Not when only the caregiver attended. Not when only the infant attended. When both attended together through THE-TURN-STRUCTURE's contingent, bidirectional exchange.

Call this THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE: the oscillatory neural coupling between caregiver and infant nervous systems that constitutes the physical realization of THE-TURN-STRUCTURE. THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE is not a metaphor for "closeness" or "attunement." It is a measurable inter-brain correlation pattern in specific frequency bands (theta, associated with hippocampal encoding) that co-occurs with contingent conversational exchange and predicts vocabulary outcomes, independently of word-count.

THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE has a specific implication for what electronic toys eliminate. When an electronic toy occupies both sides of the exchange — producing both the initiating stimulus and the resolving response — it does not just reduce THE-TURN-STRUCTURE's behavioral frequency. It eliminates the condition under which THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE forms. Two nervous systems synchronized to a shared external object (the toy's audio output) are not building THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE between themselves. They are each independently synchronized to a machine. The coupling is not dyadic; it is two parallel monoadic couplings to the same external source. The neural synchrony that constitutes THE-TURN-STRUCTURE's developmental yield is absent.

The prior document's P4 prediction — that children playing with traditional toys will direct more gaze toward the parent's face than children playing with electronic toys — now has a neural substrate. Face-directed gaze initiates the communicative exchange that establishes THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE. The reduction in face-directed gaze is the behavioral signature of a reduction in inter-brain synchrony events. THE-PREDICTION-SURFACE-SCAN from the prior document's predictions, designed to test P4, is now also a test of THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE hypothesis.

## VII. THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION: Curiosity as a Prior Transmitted Through THE-TURN-STRUCTURE

THE-TURN-STRUCTURE transmits language. THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE synchronizes neural oscillations. A third thing THE-TURN-STRUCTURE transmits, previously unnamed in this lineage, is epistemic stance: the parent's own curiosity signals are transmitted to the child as prior updates about the information-value of the environment.

Varga and Kovács (2026; Scientific Reports) tested whether 14-month-old infants attribute information-seeking goals to other agents — whether they understand others as seekers of information driven by uncertainty or curiosity. The result: infants in the first year of life already attribute epistemic goals to others, evaluating the efficiency of others' information-seeking actions. They distinguish an agent who seeks information efficiently from one who seeks inefficiently, and they track whether others' uncertainty-driven exploration was resolved. The infant's epistemic sense — its model of others as information-seeking agents — is operational before the end of the first year.

The implication: when a parent says "I wonder if that will hold," the infant is not merely hearing a sentence. It is receiving a signal from a modeled agent whose epistemic state it already tracks — a signal that updates the infant's prior about the information-value of the object the parent is wondering about. The parent's wonder is transmitted as a curiosity signal, which shifts the infant's THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT toward that object, increasing the probability that the infant will invest attention in generating and resolving prediction errors about it.

Call this THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION: the transmission of curiosity — operationalized as an update to the infant's prior about the information-value of an object or configuration — through THE-TURN-STRUCTURE's contingent exchange. THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION is not about verbal content. A parent who names an object provides word-object mappings. A parent who marks their own uncertainty about an object provides something more structurally significant: a prior update that the object is inside THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT and worth sustained exploration.

THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION has a specific implication for electronic toy design. A toy that pre-resolves the prediction error also pre-resolves the parent's uncertainty — or rather, eliminates it entirely, since the toy's outcome is determined. A parent playing with an electronic toy has nothing to wonder about; the outcome is announced before the question can form. THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION cannot fire. The child receives no curiosity signal from the parent, because the toy's scripted resolution has eliminated the epistemic state the parent would have needed to transmit it.

The complement of this is that a parent playing with a genuinely uncertain construction environment — one where "I wonder if that will hold" is an authentic epistemic state — is performing THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION continuously, regardless of whether any specific verbal content is delivered. The uncertainty in the parent's voice, the direction of their gaze toward the unstable structure, the slight pause before acting — all of these are epistemic signals that Varga and Kovács (2026) show 14-month-olds already track and attribute to an agent's curiosity state. The parent-as-environment framing from the prior document now has a mechanism: THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION is how the parent's curiosity becomes part of the child's information environment.

## VIII. THE-RISKY-SURPLUS: Physical Affordance Uncertainty and Arousal Calibration

The prior document introduced THE-DIRECTED-SURPLUS as the increment of self-directed executive capacity accruing from environments where the child determines what counts as progress. A related but formally distinct construct has emerged from the 2025–2026 outdoor play literature: the developmental yield accruing specifically from environments where the consequence of action on the body is uncertain. Call this THE-RISKY-SURPLUS.

Gray, Down, Mann, Barnes, Sturges, Eager, and colleagues (2025; Behavioral Sciences) conducted a PRISMA-compliant scoping review of empirical studies from 2015–2025 on risky outdoor play and adventure education. Of 5,218 initial references, 40 met inclusion criteria. All 40 studies reported positive associations. The modal finding was not a single outcome but a cluster: resilience and confidence, wellbeing, physical skills, autonomy and agency, nature connectedness. The key mechanism identified across studies was authentic child agency — not the presence of physical risk per se, but the condition in which the child chooses and controls the risk-taking. This is THE-DIRECTED-SURPLUS applied to the physical domain.

The anxiety-calibration hypothesis (Dodd, University of Exeter; cited in the 2025 Nature News Feature on risky play) adds a neural mechanism: risky play produces physiological arousal — elevated heart rate, adrenaline — in a context where the child understands the arousal as chosen and controllable. Over repeated episodes, the child learns that arousal is not equivalent to danger; that the physiological signal of excitement is manageable and modifiable through action. This calibration of the arousal signal — learning to tolerate and modulate the physiological response to challenge — is the mechanism by which risky play buffers against anxiety. The 2025 Position Statement on Active Outdoor Play (Outdoor Play Canada, International Journal of Behavioral Nutrition and Physical Activity) identifies this buffering as one of the primary mechanisms by which child-led outdoor play counteracts the neurodevelopmental effects of toxic stress.

THE-RISKY-SURPLUS connects to THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT in a specific way: risky play is play in which the consequence of action on the child's own body is uncertain but tractable. The child does not know whether they will reach the branch, but they can estimate the probability and adjust their approach. The physical affordance of height, speed, or instability generates prediction errors about the body's own capabilities — proprioceptive prediction errors, not just environmental ones. THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT applies to proprioceptive prediction error as directly as it applies to environmental configuration prediction error.

An electronic toy generates no proprioceptive prediction error at all. The child's body is passive; the toy produces the outcome without requiring the child's body to manage uncertainty about its own physical capabilities. Over time, a diet of low-proprioceptive-uncertainty toys produces a child whose body has been denied the calibration experience that THE-RISKY-SURPLUS theory predicts should be accruing. The 2025 survey finding that 40% of parents feel nervous when their child climbs "too high" is the adult socialization failure that the Sandseter line of research (2026, Journal of Environmental Psychology) describes: restrictive adult responses to children's risky play eliminate THE-RISKY-SURPLUS by interrupting the child's chosen, controlled risk-taking before the calibration cycle can complete.

## IX. THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION: How Pretend Play Expands the Configuration Space Beyond the Physical Object

THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY, as defined in the prior document, is the Shannon entropy of the space of configurations a child can reach through physical manipulation of a set's parts within a session. This is the right measure for constructive play. For pretend play, it is incomplete.

Pretend play does not operate on the physical configuration space of the object. It operates on the propositional configuration space of the hypothetical world the child has instantiated. "This block is a spaceship" does not change the block's physical affordances. It changes the child's internal model of what the block affords within a posited hypothetical world. The spaceship affords different actions than the block: it can travel through space, it can fire weapons, it needs fuel. The propositional configuration space of the hypothetical world has a THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY of its own — defined over the distinguishable states the child can reach within the hypothetical world through the narrative actions available to it.

Call this THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION: the mechanism by which pretend play extends the effective THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY of a play session beyond the physical configuration space of the available objects, by adding the propositional configuration space of hypothetical worlds the child can instantiate from those objects. A single block set of ten parts has a physical THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY measurable in the thousands of states. The same block set, used in pretend play, has an effective THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY that is essentially unbounded — limited only by the child's generative model of the possible hypothetical worlds in which those physical parts can participate.

THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION has a specific implication for what the electronic toy market sells. A toy with a single correct mode of operation cannot support THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION, because the toy's pre-scripted outcomes conflict with the hypothetical world-model the child would need to construct. If the car always makes a beeping sound when it moves, the child cannot construct a hypothetical world in which the car is a sleeping beast who moves silently. THE-RESOLUTION-RACE is not just a problem for physical configuration entropy; it is a direct constraint on THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION. The toy that always produces its scripted outcome has foreclosed the narrative space that pretend play would have opened.

Conversely, a physical object without any pre-scripted outputs — the stick, the stone, the block — can participate in an unlimited number of hypothetical world-models, because it never produces an outcome that contradicts the current hypothetical. THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION of a stick is bounded only by the child's imagination. The toy's "zero internal assembly" is, in the context of THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION, its greatest asset.

## X. The Brushe Quantification: What THE-TURN-STRUCTURE Displacement Actually Costs in Information Units

The prior document cited Brushe et al. (2024) as establishing the screen-time/language-environment link. The full quantification from that study has not been made explicit in the playtritionism lineage.

Brushe and colleagues tracked 220 Australian families longitudinally from when children were 12 to 36 months, using LENA devices in 16-hour home recording sessions at 6 time points. For every minute of daily screen time at 36 months, children experienced statistically significant reductions in adult word count, child vocalizations, and conversational turn count. The researchers then calculated the cumulative daily deficit at observed versus guideline levels:

At the WHO-recommended one hour of screen time per day at 36 months: children were estimated to miss 397 adult words, 294 child vocalizations, and 68 conversational turns per day compared to a zero-screen-time baseline.

At the observed average of 172 minutes per day at 36 months: the estimated daily deficit was 1,139 adult words, 843 child vocalizations, and 194 conversational turns.

The prior document framed this as THE-TURN-STRUCTURE displacement. The Brushe quantification allows it to be expressed as an information-theoretic quantity: 194 lost conversational turns per day is 194 lost opportunities for THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE formation, 194 lost opportunities for THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION, and 194 lost opportunities for THE-TURN-STRUCTURE to generate the word-referent mappings that vocabulary research identifies as the primary mechanism of early lexical development.

The Tulviste, Tulviste, and Tamm (2026; Frontiers in Developmental Psychology) study adds a precision the prior document lacked: low screen time alone does not predict stronger language outcomes. It is low screen time combined with high conversational engagement that produces the outcome advantage. The family typology analysis in the 2026 paper identifies a group with low screen time but also low conversational engagement, and this group shows no language advantage over high-screen-time families. This is the nutrition parallel: removing the harmful item is necessary but not sufficient if the nutrient it was displacing was never present in the first place. THE-TURN-STRUCTURE is not restored by removing the screen; it must be actively generated by a caregiver present and willing to respond contingently. The toy does not crowd out only the screen; it crowds out the caregiver's epistemic presence.

## XI. The Sleep-Play Correspondence: Why THE-WAKE-WINDOW Reframes the Bedtime Story

The Van Roy and King (2026) finding — that children consolidate motor learning during wakefulness, unlike adults who require sleep — has a corollary that the playtritionism argument should name explicitly.

If children's hippocampal consolidation of play-generated learning occurs during awake rest following active play episodes, then the temporal structure of the day matters in a way the toy-market literature has never addressed. A child who moves directly from an active play episode to a screen-based passive entertainment episode — with no unstructured rest between them — is experiencing a disrupted THE-WAKE-WINDOW. The hippocampal replay that would integrate the preceding episode's prediction-error signals into the generative model is interrupted by a stream of new external stimulation that competes for the same neural resources.

The bedtime story, by contrast, is the optimal THE-WAKE-WINDOW intervention: it provides low-arousal, predictable external structure that does not generate strong new prediction errors of its own, creating the conditions under which post-play hippocampal consolidation can complete. A bedtime story is not developmental primarily because of the vocabulary in the text. It is developmental because it creates a THE-WAKE-WINDOW that allows the day's play-generated learning to consolidate. The content matters less than the structure: low external stimulation, predictable narrative arc, contingent adult presence (the caregiver reading aloud is performing THE-TURN-STRUCTURE implicitly, even without explicit turn-taking).

This is a previously untheorized mechanism by which the transition to screen-based entertainment at bedtime could be specifically harmful — not because of the screen's content, but because it generates high-novelty prediction errors that compete with hippocampal replay of the day's play-generated learning.

## Signal

The playtritionism frame has now passed through three documents. Each sharpened a prior vagueness into something testable. Document One named the matrix. Document Two formalized it and introduced prediction error as the mechanism. Document Three fills the remaining gaps:

THE-GOLDILOCKS-GRADIENT is the functional form THE-RESOLVABLE-UNCERTAINTY-WINDOW was always describing: an inverted-U over complexity, with the peak defined by the child's current generative model and the baseline defined by their THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR. Electronic toys with fixed outputs push the child permanently to the left side of the curve — below the window — after a small number of interactions. A loose-parts set of appropriate complexity maintains the session inside the window continuously.

THE-CURIOSITY-VECTOR is the individual-difference moderator that explains why the same block set produces different developmental yields across children of the same age. It is measurable at 8 months and predictive of IQ at 3.5 years. No toy label acknowledges it.

THE-IMAGINATION-WAS-ALWAYS-THE-INFERENCE names the connection between pretend play and the active inference framework: pretend play is the generative model running offline, in hypothetical mode, producing and resolving counterfactual prediction errors. A child whose physical play has been pre-resolved by scripted electronic outcomes has a generative model too thin to support rich pretend play.

THE-WAKE-WINDOW identifies the post-play awake rest period during which children's hippocampal circuits consolidate play-generated learning — a mechanism operating on different timescales in children than in adults, and eliminated by continuous-stimulation electronic toys that fill every pause with a new prompt.

THE-SYNCHRONY-SUBSTRATE names what THE-TURN-STRUCTURE actually is at the neural level: inter-brain oscillatory coupling in the temporoparietal regions that support language, measurable via hyperscanning, eliminated by any toy that occupies both sides of the contingent exchange simultaneously.

THE-EPISTEMIC-CONTAGION names the mechanism by which the parent's curiosity becomes part of the child's information environment: the transmission of epistemic priors through THE-TURN-STRUCTURE, operationalized as updates to the infant's model of the information-value of the shared object. A toy that pre-resolves every question eliminates the parent's epistemic state and thereby eliminates the signal.

THE-RISKY-SURPLUS names the subset of THE-DIRECTED-SURPLUS accruing from uncertain physical consequence: the calibration of proprioceptive prediction error and physiological arousal that only child-chosen, child-managed physical risk can provide.

THE-COUNTERFACTUAL-EXTENSION names the mechanism by which pretend play extends THE-CONFIGURATION-ENTROPY beyond the physical into the propositional: the child opening a hypothetical configuration space from a physically simple object, generating and resolving narrative prediction errors, and thereby accessing a developmental yield that no physical entropy measure would have predicted.

None of these quantities appear on a label. All of them are now measurable. The experiments that would test them directly have been proposed here. THE-CAUSAL-HORIZON remains.

The signal is the same one it has always been, now more precisely located:

The child with the block is doing something more sophisticated than any educational product has ever described. They are running a generative model of physical reality, updating it through action, pausing to consolidate the updates, synchronizing their neural oscillations with the adult who is equally uncertain beside them, receiving the adult's curiosity as a prior update, extending the model into hypothetical space through pretend play, and calibrating their body's response to physical uncertainty through the risk they choose and manage themselves.

None of that requires wood. None of it requires seven colors. None of it requires a pre-recorded voice announcing a resolution before the question has formed. None of it fits on a label.

Put the block on the floor. Sit with genuine uncertainty about what it will become. Let the child see that uncertainty in your face. Step back when they reach for it. Let the pause happen. Answer when asked, and only then. The curriculum was always the curiosity. The curiosity was always the curriculum.
